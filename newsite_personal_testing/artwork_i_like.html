Jean Baptiste Camille Corot
Corot is the complete master of the landscape. Corot often painted numerous depictions of the same location, emphasizing different aspects in each variation as mood or memory struck him. As he told his biographer Théophile Silvestre, “When a collector wants a repetition of one of my landscapes, it is easy for me to give it to him without seeing the original: I keep a copy of all my works in my heart and in my eyes.”


“I hope with all my heart there will be painting in heaven” – Jean Baptiste Camille Corot (1796-1875)
Giovanni Fattori
“Il vero non ha bisogno di soggetti bizzarri, il vero è così bello che l’artista trova tutto nella vita che gli si muove attorno.”
"The truth has no need for bizarre subjects; the truth is so beautiful that the artist finds everything in the life that moves around him." - Giovanni Fattori (1825-1908)
Charles Csuri (1922-2022)
Charles Csuri was an early generative artist (creating his first digital art pieces in 1964). I find his work a lot more compelling than his contemporaries also working with computer art at the time (Molnar, etc) - maybe it's because unlike most other generative artists at the time he came from a background of traditional painting.
Nam June Paik
Nam June Paik's work will always hold a very special place for me, one of his shows was the first art show which I saw growing up that I really felt a connection with.
"Nature is beautiful, not because it changes beautifully, but simply because it changes." - Nam June Paik (1932-2006)
