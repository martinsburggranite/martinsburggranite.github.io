A visit to the Church of Our Lady Of Kazan in Moisenay

In the spring of 2026, I had the great privelege to go and attend liturgy the Church of Our Lady Of Kazan (Eglise de Notre Dame de Kazan) in Moisenay, France.

The building itself was designed and built solely by Archimandrite Euthyme (Wendt), constructed using stones from the surrounding farmland. The church was consecrated in 1964, and has celebrating the divine liturgy regularly ever since.

More information about the Church and it's history is available on the Church's website (in French): https://skite-notredame-de-kazan.blogspot.com/p/histoire-de-lermitage.html

In the early 1960's Fr. Gregory Kroug painted a full series of frescoes on the walls and ceilings.

On the left is the main view that you see inside the church - the frescoes were executed in the early 1960's by Fr. Gregory Kroug (+1969) - one of the foremost iconographers associated with the "Rediscovery of the Icon" (Leonid Ouspensky is also one of the most important iconographers of the 20th century, him and Fr. Gregory were lifelong friends). The frescoes he did here were in a rush; they are not actually proper 'frescoes', in the sense that they are done while the cement is wet so that the pigment binds into the material itself, since they are all egg tempera (the normal technique for painting icons on boards). They require a lot of care, and have undergone restoration.
